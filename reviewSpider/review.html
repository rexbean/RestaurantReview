      Specialties

      We serve up soul food that is soul good! Our restaurant and staff make you feel right at home, from the food, to the furniture, and to the people. We serve food like your momma would make, or food you wish your momma had made! Our relaxed environment is perfect for family meals or catching up with friends, and our living room is perfect for enjoying the game. We even have an outdoor patio that is a wonderful place for sipping some sweet tea, a beer, or our famous Ghetto Grape Kool Aid Mimosa.

      Our delicious soul food includes favorites such as smoked barbecue chicken, fried chicken, fried catfish, barbecue ribs, smoked brisket, hot links, and chicken and waffles. Our sides are equally as good, such as cornbread, hushpuppies, collard greens, fried okra, coleslaw, baked mac and cheese, baked beans, fries and sweet potato fries, potato salad, and corn on the cob.

      Join us on Sunday for our all-you-can-eat Sunday brunch! There's not a better brunch value in all of the Bay Area.

      History

      Established in 2010.

      Our mission statement is to value and serve each of our customers, and provide a down-home quality cooked product, while cultivating an environment of friendliness, integrity, and laughter. We hope to continually grow spiritually, mentally, emotionally, physically, and financially according to the will of God, while empowering our employees and customers to do the same.

      Meet the Business Owner

      Rhonda M.
      Business Owner

      As a child, Chef Rhonda was coaxed into the kitchen by Grandma Lillie Mae Gaulden, and her mom, Peggy Hoffman Gaulden. These women were the best cooks anywhere and everyone knew it. Chef Rhonda recollects that her grandmother and mom would frequently call for her to get things from the pantry or fridge.

      She soon learned that this was their way of teaching her without telling her that class was in session. She started to pay attention to the ingredients Granny and Mom would put in their favorite dishes, and before long, she started to learn recipes and history of one of Americas treasures, soul food.
